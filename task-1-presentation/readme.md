As a Student Digital Ambassador, I designed a one-page visual resource to help my batchmates understand the significance of digital literacy and why it matters in our academic journey. For this task, I used Canva, a free web-based design tool, to create the infographic. My design covers three essential modules: a definition of digital literacy, a curated list of digital tools for students, and critical safe internet practices.I chose a professional blue-and-white color palette to ensure the infographic looked authoritative yet accessible for a college audience. What I found most interesting while creating this design was the process of "information distillation" taking broad, complex subjects like cybersecurity and professional etiquette and condensing them into brief, high-impact visual points. It was a challenging but rewarding exercise to maintain a clean visual hierarchy, ensuring that critical details, such as the National Cyber Crime Helpline (1930), were prominent without cluttering the layout. This task helped me realize that effective digital communication is as much about design as it is about information.
